    My Life as a Windows User

    My family had a desktop computer and I remember smashing keys on Microsoft Word. That's probably the first memory I can think of when thinking about technology. I don't think I could even spell anything at this point. I'd entertain myself by playing games like the pre-installed Minesweeper, which I still don't know how to play. I'd waste hours playing Pinball and trying to beat my own score. And I'd get my first exposure to digital art by trying to draw on MS Paint. I think this just normalized technology for me; I didn't realize some people had their first experience much later in life.

    First memories of using a computer

    Hello Kitty

    At this point, I listened to what anyone told me irregardless of who it was. I saw commercials that told me to ask a parent before going online. I would ask my dad if I could go on hellokitty.com, All I would ever do on this website is make wishlists of merchandise I wish I could have bought. At this point, I was connecting things I liked in reality (like Pokemon and TV channel's websites) and finding the sort of "enhancements" or extras to the things I already liked. I'd go and play flash games, but it wasn't massively multiplayer, it was just me.

    I would listen to anything the TV told me

    The Neopets Era

    Like any other kid in my class, I enjoyed Neopets. I had my first exposure to HTML and CSS when trying to make my userlookup look really good, but I still had no idea what I was doing. I joined guilds and tried to raise in the ranks and try to be one of the higher levels and talk to others. It was probably one of my first times talking to strangers on the Internet (and trying to impress them?). I still spend too much time on this website.

    Making online friends

    This is kind of a very generalized section, which encompasses a lot years that were really formative for me. I joined other sites, became a moderator, and was actually was a person of authority when I was in 6th grade. I was on websites like deviantART and had people interested in being my friend and making art with me. I played MMORPGs and made friends as we all made virtual money and powered up our virtual avatars. I made a lot of friends and it began to open the world to me. It was still a narrow view but wider than I think it could've ever been to me at that time.

    I still keep in touch with them

    Descent

    I joined Tumblr after an online friend showed it to me. All I ever did was look at bad memes. I think this was a descent of me going from a creative and productive person into someone more passive and only regenerating content from other sources. This is valid too, but it was definitely a shift for me as a user.
